Called to Thrive Entrepreneurship Training Report

Silver Level Program Analysis & Recommendations

12 Sessions from May 24 to October 25

Executive Summary

HigherLife Foundation
Children with Hope

The Called to Thrive Silver Level Entrepreneurship Training was a joint initiative between HigherLife Foundation Lifelong Development in partnership with Children with Hope. The program was delivered online to 240 registered participants from Zimbabwe (226) and Lesotho (14).

Program Objective

The primary objective was to equip young HLF fellows with interest in entrepreneurship - who either have existing businesses or are keen to start - with the essential skills needed to thrive in their entrepreneurial journeys.

Critical Finding: While the program achieved an average of 86 qualified attendees per session, active engagement was extremely low. Only approximately 20 participants consistently engaged in verbal discussions, with 30-40 using the chat feature. The majority of qualified attendees remained passive observers despite being present in sessions.

Critical Engagement Challenge

Severe Participation Gap: Despite having 86 qualified attendees on average, only approximately 20 participants (23%) actively engaged in verbal discussions. This represents a significant disconnect between attendance and active participation.

Breakout Room Challenges: Many breakout rooms experienced limited verbal participation, with participants either:

  • Keeping cameras off and microphones muted
  • Not responding to facilitator prompts verbally
  • Relying on the core 20 participants to carry out discussions

Chat vs Verbal Participation: While 30-40 participants used chat features, this still represents less than half of qualified attendees actively contributing in any format.

Critical Challenges Identified

1. Severe Verbal Engagement Gap

Only 23% of qualified attendees participated verbally in discussions or breakout rooms. The majority remained silent observers despite being present throughout sessions.

2. Breakout Room Silence

Multiple breakout rooms experienced limited verbal participation, with heavy reliance on the core 20 engaged participants to carry discussions.

3. Small Core Engagement Group

Only approximately 20 participants formed the consistently engaged core group, creating an imbalance where a small minority carried discussion while the majority remained silent.

4. Passive Learning Culture

The program struggled to transition participants from passive listeners to active collaborators, limiting the potential for peer learning and practical skill development.

Positive Outcomes

Dedicated Core Group

Despite engagement challenges, a core group of approximately 20 participants demonstrated consistent commitment and active verbal participation throughout the program.

Chat Engagement

30-40 participants regularly used chat features to ask questions and contribute, showing some level of engagement even without verbal participation.

Udemy License Distribution

25 participants received Udemy licenses for technical courses, providing valuable supplementary learning resources.

Strong Attendance Commitment

86 average qualified attendees indicates significant interest and commitment to the program's content and schedule.

Recommendations for Future Implementation

1. Restructure Group Size and Delivery

Limit cohorts to 30-40 participants for optimal interaction. Run multiple smaller cohorts rather than one large group.

2. Enhance Curriculum with Practical Content

Add technical modules on financial management, digital marketing execution, sales, business operations, and legal basics.

3. Increase Session Interactivity

Incorporate breakout activities with clear deliverables, live demonstrations, peer review sessions, and weekly challenges.

4. Implement Better Engagement Strategies

Use pre-work assignments, structured breakout rooms, gamification, and post-session accountability mechanisms.

5. Leverage Udemy Licenses More Strategically

Assign specific Udemy modules as prerequisites, create blended learning pathways, and track completion rates.

Conclusion

The Called to Thrive Entrepreneurship Training program successfully attracted significant interest with an average of 86 qualified attendees per session. However, the program faced a critical challenge in translating attendance into active engagement, with only approximately 20 participants consistently contributing to verbal discussions and 30-40 using chat features.

Key Takeaway: Future program success depends on fundamentally redesigning the engagement approach to transform passive attendees into active participants. The focus must shift from measuring attendance to measuring meaningful participation, with specific strategies to address the verbal participation gap that limited collaborative learning in this cohort.
