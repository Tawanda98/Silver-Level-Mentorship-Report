Called to Thrive Entrepreneurship Training Program Report

Silver Level Program Analysis & Recommendations

12 Sessions from May 24 to October 25

Executive Summary

HigherLife Foundation
Children with Hope

The Called to Thrive Silver Level Entrepreneurship Training program was a joint initiative between HigherLife Foundation Lifelong Development in partnership with Children with Hope. The program was delivered online to 240 registered participants from Zimbabwe (226) and Lesotho (14).

Program Objective

The primary objective was to equip young HLF fellows with interest in entrepreneurship - who either have existing businesses or are keen to start - with the essential skills needed to thrive in their entrepreneurial journeys.

While the program successfully attracted a diverse group of young entrepreneurs aged 18-30, several implementation challenges emerged that impacted overall engagement and learning outcomes.

Key Finding: Average attendance ranged from 50-60 participants, with peaks of 80 and lows of 34, indicating significant drop-off from the initial registration numbers.

Critical Challenges Identified

1. Low Participation and Engagement

Participants were reluctant to engage in small group discussions, with limited peer-to-peer interaction reducing collaborative learning opportunities.

2. Group Size Management

The cohort of 240 participants was too large for effective online delivery, making meaningful discussions difficult and reducing personalized attention.

3. Curriculum Limitations

The Called to Thrive material, while spiritually grounded, lacked practical entrepreneurial application with minimal technical skills training.

4. Session Interactivity

Sessions were primarily lecture-based with insufficient use of interactive tools, polls, quizzes, or hands-on activities.

Positive Outcomes

Udemy License Distribution

25 participants received Udemy licenses for three key courses: Entrepreneurship fundamentals, Digital marketing, and Content creation.

Diverse Business Representation

The program successfully attracted entrepreneurs across five major sectors, creating potential for cross-sector learning and collaboration.

Gender Balance

With 53% female participation, the program demonstrated strong appeal to women entrepreneurs, crucial for inclusive economic development.

Recommendations for Future Implementation

1. Restructure Group Size and Delivery

Limit cohorts to 30-40 participants for optimal interaction. Run multiple smaller cohorts rather than one large group.

2. Enhance Curriculum with Practical Content

Add technical modules on financial management, digital marketing execution, sales, business operations, and legal basics.

3. Increase Session Interactivity

Incorporate breakout activities with clear deliverables, live demonstrations, peer review sessions, and weekly challenges.

4. Implement Better Engagement Strategies

Use pre-work assignments, structured breakout rooms, gamification, and post-session accountability mechanisms.

5. Leverage Udemy Licenses More Strategically

Assign specific Udemy modules as prerequisites, create blended learning pathways, and track completion rates.

Conclusion

The Called to Thrive Entrepreneurship Training program successfully attracted a diverse and ambitious group of young entrepreneurs across multiple sectors. However, structural challenges—including an oversized cohort, insufficient interactivity, and a theory-heavy curriculum lacking technical depth—limited participant engagement and practical skill development.

Key Takeaway: Future iterations should focus on smaller, more interactive cohorts with a redesigned curriculum that blends values-based entrepreneurship education with actionable technical skills, ensuring participants leave not just inspired, but equipped to grow their businesses.
